A Tale of Two Power Bases Two distinct archetypes prevailed at recent Eternal competitive events.

Let's take a look at their power bases using the Eternal Power Calculator:

Rakano Valkyries - ETS First-Place Finisher





Tobboo piloted Rakano Valkyries to a first place finish at RNG's Eternal Tournament Series S3W3, showcasing a new breed of Midrange  lists  made possible in large part due to a couple of exciting new Power Base tools introduced with the Fall of Argenport.

It is no secret that Icaria the LIberator wins games but...although she is in Rakano colors, she has most typically been played as a build-around finisher in 3 color Control decks that splash  or .

From a lore perspective, a pure and proactive  deck might seem more fitting for the leading Rebel Valkyrie . But the aggressive slant of Rakano's early game has typically necessitated a 3rd color to compensate for the lack of card draw and power gain needed to reliably play a 7-cost Legendary with Influence requirements.

That appears to be changing now with with some help from a Crest and a Gunslinger Mage.  BulletshaperAnticipation for the Crest of Glory has been on-going since Crests were first introduced during The Dusk Road, and with the release of Fall of Argenport they are finally here. Not suprisingly a full set is included in Toboo's list.

But it is an unsung 2-cost unit that has unexpectedly made big Icaria even more playable. Not only does Bulletshaper immediately provide much needed  on Summon, he also has the ability to grant a +2 Power boost that potentially enables Icaria as early as Turn 5. 

Bulletshaper x4 is a big part of the reason why Tobboo's total number for  and sources in this deck comes to a whopping 25 and 35 respectively.

Onward to GloryIt can be challenging for many decks to hit triple Influence even in one color. But the Power Calculator shows us that  Tobboo can quite comfortably expect to draw enough Influence to play any of his cards on curve, including both  and .

When it comes to drawing Influence, Tobboo's lowest probability for the entire deck is 88%  - and the rest reach well into the 90s.

Although Power Cards are kept to the minimum of 25, a combination of Power-drawing units and spells bring the total number of Power sources in Tobboo's list to 37. 

Bulletshaper, Seek Power, Icaria: Valkyrie Captain, and Privilege of Rank all combine forces to make Rakano Valkyries one of the most reliable Power Bases imaginable, and this undoubtedly contributed to Tobboo's first place finish in the ETS.

While Tobbo’s Rakano Valkyries demonstrates a new Midrange archetype for a faction pair typically associated with more aggressive decks, Isochron’s Adapted Green Feln presents a fresh take on a classic Control-oriented approach.

What first stands out on the Influence Chart are low percentages for Primal, dipping to 64% for and 59% for .

Numbers this low would be almost unthinkable in nearly any Aggro or Midrange deck. However, a Control build like this one can afford it thanks to an abundance of filtering and card draw.

Although Isochron has chosen to include only one additional Power Card beyond the minimum of 25, Adapted Green Feln features no less than 14 card draw effects including Seek Power.

Assuming Isochron is able to play at least two of these before Turn 7, their chances of drawing enough Influence to play Channel the Tempest on curve approach a more comfortable 80%. (See columns 10-12 on the chart - these represent 10-12 draws beyond the opening hand when going first).
